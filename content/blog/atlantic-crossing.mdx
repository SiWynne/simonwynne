There's a particular kind of quiet in the middle of an ocean crossing — no
signal, no schedule, nothing to do except deal with whatever the day brings.

## Plans are a starting point, not a promise

Weather doesn't care about your itinerary. Neither does most of life. The
sooner you treat a plan as a best guess rather than a contract, the less
everything derails you.

## Small crews reveal character fast

There's nowhere to hide on a boat. Whatever someone's like under pressure,
you'll know within a week. It made me think differently about who I choose to
work with on land, too.
