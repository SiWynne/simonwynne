Most people don't fail because they lack ideas. They fail because they stop
two-thirds of the way through, right when the work stops being fun and starts
being work.

## The messy middle

Every project has a point where the initial excitement is gone and the finish
line still isn't visible. That's not a sign something's wrong — it's the
default state of doing anything worthwhile.

The people who finish aren't more talented. They've just made peace with the
messy middle, and they've built habits that don't depend on motivation to keep
moving.

## What actually helps

- Ship smaller pieces, more often — momentum compounds
- Separate "make it" from "make it good" — editing a bad draft is easier than
  writing a good one from nothing
- Decide in advance what "done" looks like, so you're not negotiating with
  yourself every day

None of this is complicated. It's just uncomfortable, which is exactly why
most people skip it.
